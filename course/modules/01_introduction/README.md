# Module 1: Introduction to Cardiovascular Emergencies

## Overview
This module provides a comprehensive introduction to cardiovascular emergencies, covering the fundamental anatomy, physiology, and pathophysiology essential for understanding cardiac emergency care.

## Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will be able to:
- Describe the normal anatomy and physiology of the cardiovascular system
- Identify key risk factors for cardiovascular disease
- Recognize early signs and symptoms of cardiac emergencies
- Understand the importance of rapid assessment and intervention

## Module Structure

### 1.1 Cardiovascular Anatomy and Physiology (2 hours)
- Heart structure and function
- Electrical conduction system
- Coronary circulation
- Cardiac cycle and hemodynamics

### 1.2 Pathophysiology of Cardiac Emergencies (2 hours)
- Ischemia, injury, and infarction
- Arrhythmia mechanisms
- Heart failure pathophysiology
- Shock states

### 1.3 Risk Factors and Prevention (2 hours)
- Modifiable and non-modifiable risk factors
- Primary and secondary prevention strategies
- Risk stratification tools
- Patient education principles

### 1.4 Recognition and Initial Assessment (2 hours)
- Chief complaints in cardiac emergencies
- Focused history taking
- Physical examination techniques
- Triage and prioritization
