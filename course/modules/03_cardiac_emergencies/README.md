# Module 3: Cardiac Emergency Management

## Overview
This module covers the practical management of cardiac emergencies, including evidence-based protocols, pharmacological interventions, and procedural skills essential for emergency care.

## Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, you will be able to:
- Apply ACLS protocols in cardiac arrest situations
- Manage acute coronary syndromes appropriately
- Recognize and treat life-threatening arrhythmias
- Demonstrate competence in emergency cardiac procedures

## Module Structure

### 3.1 Cardiac Arrest and ACLS (4 hours)
- CPR quality and mechanics
- Defibrillation and cardioversion
- ACLS algorithms and protocols
- Post-resuscitation care

### 3.2 Acute Coronary Syndromes (4 hours)
- STEMI management and reperfusion strategies
- NSTEMI/UA risk stratification and treatment
- Antiplatelet and anticoagulation therapy
- Interventional vs. medical management

### 3.3 Arrhythmia Management (4 hours)
- Stable vs. unstable tachycardia
- Bradycardia management
- Atrial fibrillation with RVR
- Ventricular arrhythmia treatment

### 3.4 Cardiogenic Shock and Heart Failure (3 hours)
- Hemodynamic assessment
- Inotropic and vasopressor therapy
- Mechanical circulatory support
- Acute decompensated heart failure

## Emergency Procedures
- Synchronized cardioversion
- Transcutaneous pacing
- Central line access
- Intra-aortic balloon pump basics

## Pharmacology Focus
- Emergency cardiac medications
- Dosing and administration routes
- Contraindications and side effects
- Drug interactions and considerations

## Assessment
