# CVE - Cardiovascular Emergencies with ECG Course

## Course Overview
Welcome to the Cardiovascular Emergencies (CVE) course, a comprehensive training program designed for healthcare professionals focusing on ECG interpretation and emergency cardiac care management.

## Course Description
This 40-hour course provides in-depth training on recognizing, interpreting, and managing cardiovascular emergencies. Students will develop expertise in ECG analysis, emergency protocols, and evidence-based cardiac care practices essential for emergency medicine, cardiology, and critical care settings.

## Target Audience
- Emergency medicine physicians and residents
- Critical care nurses and physicians
- Paramedics and EMTs
- Medical students in clinical rotations
- Healthcare professionals seeking ACLS certification

## Course Structure

### [Module 1: Introduction to Cardiovascular Emergencies](modules/01_introduction/)
**Duration:** 8 hours  
**Focus:** Anatomy, physiology, and basic assessment principles

### [Module 2: ECG Fundamentals and Interpretation](modules/02_ecg_fundamentals/)
**Duration:** 12 hours  
**Focus:** Systematic ECG analysis and rhythm recognition

### [Module 3: Cardiac Emergency Management](modules/03_cardiac_emergencies/)
**Duration:** 15 hours  
**Focus:** ACLS protocols and emergency interventions

### [Module 4: Assessment and Case Studies](modules/04_assessment/)
**Duration:** 5 hours  
**Focus:** Comprehensive evaluation and certification

## Learning Path
```
Prerequisites Check → Module 1 → Module 2 → Module 3 → Module 4 → Certification
```

## Prerequisites
- Basic medical knowledge and terminology
- Understanding of human anatomy and physiology
- Current BLS (Basic Life Support) certification recommended

## Certification
Upon successful completion, participants will receive:
- CVE Course Completion Certificate
- 40 hours of continuing education credits
- ACLS preparation certification (where applicable)

## Technical Requirements
